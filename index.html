About

Masjid Al-Imam Al-Albani is a mosque located in Brooklyn, New York City, dedicated to teaching and practicing Islam upon the Sunnah according to the understanding of the pious predecessors (As-Salaf As-Salih).

The imam of the masjid is Shaykh Abu Dujanah, one of the early students of Dammaj in Yemen. He studied under Imam Muqbil Ibn Hadi Al-Wadi'i (May Allah have Mercy on him) for 7 years. The shaykh gives daily lessons, focusing on beneficial knowledge and adherence to the Sunnah.

Please note: Masjid Al-Imam Al-Albani currently does not have sufficient space to accommodate sisters.

Lessons

When are they?

Between Maghrib and Isha every day.

What's being taught?

Currently: Kitab At-Tawheed, Bulugh Al-Maram, and Al-Jaami' As-Saheeh.

Are there translators?

English translators are always available, while translators for other languages are only sometimes available.

Is there anything I should bring?

Ideally, one should bring a notebook and something to write with.

Are the lessons broadcasted/recorded?

Yes, live lessons can be streamed on our Mixlr, and past lessons can be found on our YouTube channel.
